	Agriculture is an important part of Bangladesh's economy as well as supporting food security and rural livelihoods. However, farmers often struggle with practical information about crop diseases, market prices, weather conditions, and effective farming practices. To address these challenges, AgriGenie was developed as an integrated smart agriculture platform where  Disease detection, weather support, market trend analysis, and a farmer-buyer marketplace are provided through a single web-based system that is easy to use.The backend of AgriGenie was implemented using Django, the user interface was developed using HTML , CSS and JavaScript, and data management was implemented through PostgreSQL. Using this web-based system, crop images can be uploaded for disease identification for crops. Direct communication between farmers and buyers is also included for helping fair price negotiation and better market decisions.Security issue and roll based accessed were handle carefully. In spite of facing several challenges during development, we successfully resolved them through rigorous testing and refining. Therefore, AgriGenie has now become a key digital system in favor of the farmers in Bangladesh, which we believe, helped them in maintaining agricultural products with great ease.

\chapter{Introduction}

\section{Introduction}

Agriculture plays a very important role in Bangladeshs economy. It helps with food security, jobs and rural livelihoods.Farmers, especially small ones in rural areas face many challenges. They struggle to use technologies and get timely information. They do not know much about crop diseases. They also face fluctuating market prices, unpredictable weather and limited access to buyers. These problems reduce their productivity and profitability.

To address these problems, AgriGenie is introduced as a smart agriculture assistance platform. Multiple services are integrated into one easy-to-use system, including crop disease detection, weather support, market price forecasting, and a farmer-buyer marketplace. In this way, precision agriculture and sustainable farming are promoted.Unlike tools that are expensive or limited AgriGenie is affordable and multifunctional. It uses web technologies and artificial intelligence. It helps achieve the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal (SDG 2: Zero Hunger).

\section{Problem Statement}

Bangladesh's agriculture sector faces several major challenges that affect productivity and sustainability. Farmers often lack effective tools for early disease detection, as a result, excessive use of chemicals that harms both crops, crops yield and the environment. In addition, limited access to reliable market price information allows intermediaries to exploit farmers, reducing their profits. Unpredictable weather conditions such as floods and droughts further increase the risk of crop loss, as farmers struggle to make informed decisions. Small-scale farmers especially face hard times in selling their crops in the market at fair price. Besides, lack of modern farming knowledge and illiteracy also adds a great burden which ultimately reduces the crops quality and production as well as their profitability. Our AgriGenie is focusing on solving all these based on a smart technology-based system with easier access to farmers.
\section{Project Objectives}

\begin{itemize}
	
	\item To develop a smart integrated system for farmers to make right decision
	
	\item To improve farmers productivity and reduce crop losses using AI tools
	
	\item To ensure reasonable prices and accessing market for farmers
	
\end{itemize}

\section{Project Scope}

AgriGenie is designed mainly for farmers, buyers and for the administrative system for Bangladesh. The system we maid aims for selected crops like potato, tomato, and bell pepper and also covers a limited scope for common crop diseases. It allows farmers to upload their crop images periodically to detect any disease, gives them weather related advices and price-based insights and also the buyers can directly converse with farmers and negotiate on crops matter. Our design ensures accessibility for rural users as well with simple interface. However, the system is only applicable for a predefined crops and their diseases and its availability greatly depends on internet connection and input data. Overall, the scope of AgriGenie lies in providing an integrated, user-centered solution for improving agricultural decision-making and market access.

\section{Novelty and Innovation}

AgriGenie is different from other agricultural platforms because it offers a complete solution. It helps farmers detect diseases, get market information and sell their crops directly to buyers. The platform uses models that're specific to Bangladesh. This means it can detect diseases that're common in this region more accurately.

AgriGenie is also designed to be easy to use. The dashboards are simple.It can be used on mobile phones. This makes it more accessible for farmers with technical knowledge. The platform also makes sure that the farmers and buyers communicate with each other at ease.

Overall AgriGenie is a platform that addresses many of the challenges faced by farmers specifically in Bangladesh. It helps farmers detect diseases predict prices and get information about the weather. This makes it an useful tool for farmers in this region. AgriGenie is a platform in the field of smart farming. It is a digital platform that integrates multiple modules to support farmers in Bangladesh.

Most agricultural platforms are either specialized or expensive. They are inaccessible to smallholder farmers in Bangladesh. Current solutions lack integration between crop disease management, market intelligence, weather alerts and direct farmer-buyer communication.

AgriGenie combines these features into a system tailored to Bangladesh. It uses datasets for disease detection and integrates price prediction using genetic algorithms.

\section{Disease Detection and Diagnosis Systems}

\subsection{PlantVillage Platform}

PlantVillage is a known platform. It helps farmers upload pictures of their crops to get help with disease diagnosis. Disease experts in farming then look at these pictures. Give advice according to that. These experts are good at sharing knowledge. However PlantVillage has some problems. It takes a time to get answers because real people have to look at each picture. PlantVillage does not have a way to automatically detect problems in time. Another issue is that the pictures and data used to train PlantVillage are not specifically about crops grown in Bangladesh. This makes it less useful for farmers in Bangladesh. The platform relies on experts for disease diagnosis. These experts provide feedback and recommendations to farmers.

PlantVillage is used for knowledge dissemination. However it needs to improve its support, for Bangladeshi crops.

\subsection{AI-Based Detection Systems}

People have been studying how artificial intelligence can help find diseases in plants. For example Kumar and his team used a kind of computer program called convolutional neural networks to figure out what was wrong with crops. They were able to do this about 94\% of the time. This is a start but these programs were only tested with information from India and they do not work well in places with limited resources. Artificial intelligence for plant disease detection is still a thing. Most systems that use intelligence for plant disease detection only look at finding the disease and do not tell farmers what to do to make the plants healthy again or work with the other tools that farmers use to take care of their farms. Artificial intelligence for plant disease detection needs to be able to do more, than just detect diseases.

\subsection{Limitations in Existing Approaches}

Existing disease detection platforms face several limitations. Such as lack of training on crops and diseases prevalent in Bangladesh, absence of treatment recommendations alongside detection weather data has no integration and adaptability is relatively poor to diverse leaf types and image quality.

AgriGenie can solve these limitations by detecting 15 plant diseases across potato, tomato, and pepper crops, giving confidence score, labeling disease types and offering proper treatment. It also generates weather alerts and integrates disease history tracking.

\section{Agricultural Price Prediction Systems}

\subsection{Agri-market Platforms}

Platforms like e-NAM in India don’t have ability to predict in the future. Some people used ARIMA to forecast and the error was approximately 8.2\%. But these models faced difficulties with massive changes in the market and unexpected situations

\subsection{Machine Learning Approaches}

By comparing three models: ARIMA, exponential smoothing and LSTM neural networks, it was found that LSTM models make mistakes 6.5\% of the time as it needs a lot of data and computer power, which farmers don’t have.
But in our AgriGenie, we use algorithm to predict the crop prices for the next 30 days. It can also find trend and gives farmer when to sell.


\section{Agricultural Marketplaces}

\subsection{Direct-to-Consumer Platforms}

In platforms like Direct2Farm and FarmDrive in Africa let farmers sell their products to the consumers. This helps to get fair price and cuts out the middlemen. However, they need to maintain product quality and have to make sure they can work in different areas.

\subsection{Challenges in Existing Marketplaces}

Current marketplaces have so many issues such as crop quality verification ,coverage diverse geographical locations ,real time communication between farmers and buyers,rating and reviews for trust 

AgriGenie handles these challenges by integrating a farmer–buyer marketplace with quality grading, buyer ratings and real-time chat. This ensure transparency, trust and fair pricing.

\section{Comprehensive Agricultural Platforms}

\subsection{ICRISAT’s Digital Advisory Services}

ICRISAT gives farmers advice on mobile phone messages directly but it is done on some limited areas and the farmers can’t give their feedback. As a result, it isn’t easy for all farmers who relies on computers.

\subsection{Regional Context: Bangladesh-Specific Systems}

Bangladesh still has limited local agricultural technology platforms. Most of the available system are adapted from other countries and do not fully reflect local crops, cultivation practices, or language needs. Therefore, a Bangladesh specific solution is required and that platform should align with local farming , it has to easy to use for farmers, support region-relevant crops, and remove language barrier.

\section{Problem Gap and Contribution Framework}

AgriGenie fills the gaps that existing agricultural technologies leave behind by bringing everything together in one place. It is an integrated platform that combines disease detection, price forecasting, a marketplace, and real time conversation all under one roof. The models are trained specifically on crops and diseases common in Bangladesh, which makes it far more useful for local farmers than anything generic. Farmers and buyers can communicate with each other directly and easily without unnecessary delays. Every type of user get their own dashboard that can be customized to suit their needs. On top of that it handles marketplace transactions, weather alerts, disease detection and price prediction together so that nothing important get missed out.

\chapter{Methodology}
\section{Introduction}

The AgriGenie system is made to be easy to use and work well for farmers. It is set up so that it can be used on different devices. The AgriGenie system has a client and a server. The client is what the farmer uses. It is made with React.js and Bootstrap. The server is made with Django. It helps with security and payments. The server also uses intelligence services. The AgriGenie system uses PostgreSQL to store information.

The AgriGenie system is made up of layers. These layers include the client, the application programming interface, the business logic, the data, the artificial intelligence services and real time communication. This makes the AgriGenie system very flexible and reliable. This part of the AgriGenie system will explain how the AgriGenie system is set up and how it works. It will talk about the AgriGenie database and the different parts of the AgriGenie system. The services include disease detection, price prediction, marketplace operations and communication. The AgriGenie system is a platform that helps farmers, with many things.

\section{System Architecture Overview}

AgriGenie has client and a server. The users can see and interacts with client. The server does all the works behind the scenes. The part that the users see is made with React.js and Bootstrap 5. This means that farmers, buyers and the people in management of the system can also use it easily. They can see all the necessary information on phones or computers. The server part is made with Django 4.2 which makes sure everything works properly without any hustle. It is the central brain of our system. It makes sure of people’s identity and also manages crops buying and selling. All the information is stored in a database called PostgreSQL which is very organized and has 38 sections with different information. AgriGenie also uses computer programs to detect disease and predict prices and also provides weather information. As a result, the farmers can get weather warnings.

All these works together like a single body with multiple layers. Each layer can be changed without affecting others and makes it more reliable and flexible. Figures~\ref{fig:system_architecture} shows how all these parts work together. This figure shows how requests, from users go through the system and how all the parts work together.

\section{Database Design}

\subsection{Entity-Relationship Model}

The AgriGenie can handle things across many parts. The main part of our database is CustomUser entity which take care of who can do what and makes sure only the right people can get in. Farmers can add their land area details, the crops they are interested to grow and payment method. Buyers can list the crops they want to buy and save it. The crops which are for sale are connected to MasterCrop templates to put all the crops in the same group. The review and rating system helps build trust between farmers and buyers. Moreover, our system also helps farmers make decision and gives them crucial information about diseases. 
Figures ~\ref{fig:ER_diagram_overview}  through ~\ref{fig:er6}  show pictures of how all the  different parts of the AgriGenie database works together. These pictures show how the CustomUser entity and other entities work together for things, like getting into the system using the panel managing farmers helping buyers and using the marketplaces and weather services.
The AgriGenie database is set up in a way that gets rid of information and makes sure everything is consistent.//

Figure~\ref{fig:er1} shows us the entity-relationship diagram of the Users and Authentication module. The CustomUser entity is based on Djangos AbstractUser. It has four different roles: the farmer role, the buyer role, the admin role and the pending role. Farmers use their phone number and a hashed PIN to authenticate. On the hand buyers use their email and password to authenticate. Each User has a one-to-one relationship with either a FarmerProfile or a BuyerProfile. It has PasswordResetToken to help buyers reset their password using their email. The Users and Authentication module also has FarmerSettings, for the farmer to set their preferences. It has FarmerPaymentMethod to save the payment accounts of the farmers. The Users and Authentication module also includes OTPVerification to help farmers recover their credentials using their phone.\\

\section{Main Parts Of AgriGenie}

\subsection{Finding Diseases In Plants}

The AgriGenie system has a way to detect diseases in plants, which uses a kind of computer model called ResNet-50 which was trained using 15000 pictures of plants and is very good at recognizing diseases. A farmer who wants to use this system, just needs to upload a picture of leaf or plant. The system then prepares the photo for the model by rearranging the size. The model upon looking at it, tries to figure out the disease. It can recognize 15 diseases in plants like potatoes, tomatoes and peppers. The model doesn’t make any guesses here. The results are shown with a score that says how sure the model is and some valuable advice to make the plant better. The system also connected to the marketplace for keeping track of the disease.

\subsection{Price Prediction Algorithm}

The Price Prediction Algorithm is used to guess the prices of things like potatoes, tomatoes and rice. The Price Prediction Algorithm looks at what happened to prices over the 10 years to make predictions. This helps it understand when prices might go up or down during times of the year.

The Price Prediction Algorithm can tell farmers what prices might be like for the 30 days. It can also say how much prices might change. The Price Prediction Algorithm is very helpful because it reduces the losses farmers have when prices change suddenly. Price prediction utilizes LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) neural networks to capture temporal dependencies in agricultural commodity prices. The equation is:

$$P_t = LSTM(P_{t-1}, P_{t-2}, \ldots, P_{t-n}) + \epsilon$$

where $P_t$ represents predicted price at time $t$ and $\epsilon$ represents prediction error. Last 10 year historical prices are used to train price predicton model.

\subsection{Recommendation System}

The AgriGenie marketplace has a recommendation system which uses a filtering process to make the user experience better. It observes what buyers likes and what crops’re like and then suggest listings that are a good match. The recommendation system is good for all walks of users. Buyers can also review things and build trust. The recommendation system is a part of AgriGenie and it help buyers and farmers in many ways. The marketplace recommendation system employs collaborative filterings, computing similarities between buyer preferences and crops characteristics. The equation is

$$\text{Similarity}(B, C) = \frac{\sum_{i} (r_{B,i} - \bar{r}_B)(w_{C,i} - \bar{w}_C)}{\sqrt{\sum_i (r_{B,i} - \bar{r}_B)^2} \cdot \sqrt{\sum_i (w_{C,i} - \bar{w}_C)^2}}$$

where $r_{B,i}$ represents buyer B's rating for crop category $i$, and $w_{C,i}$ represents weight of category $i$ for crop $C$.

AgriGenie uses a way to keep things safe and easy to use. This is called factor authentication. Farmers can register with their phone numbers and PINs. Buyers can use their email and password. There are different roles in AgriGenie and each role can do different things.

\end{itemize}

This way AgriGenie makes sure that each role can only do what it is supposed to do.
